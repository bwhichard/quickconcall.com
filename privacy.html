Privacy Policy

Effective March 22, 2026

Overview

Quick Concall is designed to be private by default. We do not collect, store, or share any personal data. Your conference call information is stored only on your device.

Data we collect

None. Quick Concall does not collect any data.

  • No analytics or usage tracking
  • No crash reporting sent to third parties
  • No advertising identifiers
  • No account or sign-in required

Data stored on your device

Quick Concall stores your conference calls (name, dial-in number, passcode, provider, notes) locally on your device in the app's private Documents folder. This data is included in iCloud and iTunes backups if you have those enabled — that is controlled entirely by you and Apple, not by us.

Third-party services

Quick Concall does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or data services. The app makes no network requests.

Children's privacy

Quick Concall does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If we ever update this policy, we will update the effective date above. Since we collect no data, changes are unlikely.
